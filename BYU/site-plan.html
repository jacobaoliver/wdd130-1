Greatest Players

BYU is known for being one of the largest producers of NFL Superstars. On this page, there will be listed every NFL who played NCAA Football at BYU. This list is based on each of these players' NFL careers, not college. There is a short description of the player and his NFL career. The header of each player is a link to their "sports-reference" page. Also, some of these players are destined to rise the list because of their young age and currently being in the NFL (Puka Nacua, Taysom Hill, Jamaal Williams, Zach Wilson, etc.). The team name next to each player was the organization who drafted the him.

1. Steve Young (QB) - 49ers

Drafted: 1st Round, 1st Overall in 1984 (USFL Draft by the LA Express, NFL Draft by Tampa Bay Buccaneers in 1985) Retired: 1999 Steve Young, a Hall of Fame quarterback, won three Super Bowls, including one as the starting QB. He was known for his athleticism, mobility, and passing accuracy, earning two NFL MVPs during his career.

2. Fred Warner (LB) - 49ers

Drafted: 3rd Round, 70th Overall in 2018 Still Active Fred Warner has quickly established himself as one of the league's top linebackers. He is known for his elite coverage skills and leadership on the 49ers' defense.

3. Jim McMahon (QB) - Bears

Drafted: 1st Round, 5th Overall in 1982 Retired: 1996 Jim McMahon led the Chicago Bears to victory in Super Bowl XX and was known for his brash personality and tough play. He earned a reputation as a gritty leader of the 1985 Bears team.

4. Kyle Van Noy (LB) - Patriots

Drafted: 2nd Round, 40th Overall in 2014 Still Active Kyle Van Noy was a critical part of the New England Patriots’ defense, contributing to multiple Super Bowl wins. He was known for his ability to rush the passer and cover tight ends.

5. Dennis Pitta (TE) - Ravens

Drafted: 4th Round, 114th Overall in 2010 Retired: 2017 Dennis Pitta was a standout tight end for the Ravens, earning Pro Bowl honors and playing a key role in their Super Bowl XLVII victory with his reliable hands and route running.

6. Ziggy Ansah (DE) - Lions

Drafted: 1st Round, 5th Overall in 2013 Retired: 2021 Ziggy Ansah was one of the top pass rushers in the league for a few years, making the Pro Bowl in 2015 and becoming the centerpiece of the Lions' defensive line.

7. Taysom Hill (QB/Utility) - Saints

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2017 Still Active Taysom Hill has thrived as a versatile player for the Saints, contributing at quarterback, tight end, and special teams, and being recognized for his unique skill set.

8. Austin Collie (WR) - Colts

Drafted: 4th Round, 127th Overall in 2009 Retired: 2013 (brief return attempt in 2016) Austin Collie was a strong wide receiver for the Indianapolis Colts, known for his route running and connection with Peyton Manning, but injuries limited his career.

9. Lee Johnson (P) - Bengals

Drafted: 7th Round, 182nd Overall in 1988 Retired: 2003 Lee Johnson had a long and productive career as a punter, earning two Pro Bowls and playing for the Bengals for over a decade.

10. Ty Detmer (QB) - 49ers

Drafted: 9th Round, 230th Overall in 1992 (49ers) Retired: 2005 Ty Detmer was a standout at BYU, earning the Heisman Trophy, though his NFL career was primarily as a backup quarterback. He played for several teams, including the Eagles and 49ers.

11. Jamaal Williams (RB) - Packers

Drafted: 4th Round, 134th Overall in 2017 Still Active Jamaal Williams has been a steady contributor at running back, known for his toughness and leadership. He’s played for the Packers and Lions, earning respect for his work ethic.

12. Vai Sikahema (KR/PR) - Eagles

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 1986 Retired: 1994 Vai Sikahema was one of the best return specialists in NFL history, making the Pro Bowl multiple times. He is known for his explosive speed and playmaking ability.

13. Todd Christensen (TE) - Raiders

Drafted: 2nd Round, 38th Overall in 1978 (Dallas Cowboys) Retired: 1988 Todd Christensen was an elite tight end, earning multiple Pro Bowl appearances and leading the NFL in receptions in 1983 while playing for the Oakland Raiders.

14. Puka Nacua (WR) - Rams

Drafted: 5th Round, 177th Overall in 2023 Still Active After a successful college career at BYU, Puka Nacua has made an immediate impact in the NFL with his athleticism and ability to make big plays as a wide receiver.

15. John Beck (QB) - Dolphins

Drafted: 2nd Round, 40th Overall in 2007 Retired: 2012 John Beck's NFL career was short-lived, primarily as a backup quarterback with the Dolphins and Washington Football Team, but he showcased flashes of potential.

16. Brady Christensen (OL) - Panthers

Drafted: 3rd Round, 70th Overall in 2021 Still Active Brady Christensen has become a reliable offensive lineman for the Carolina Panthers, contributing to both the running game and pass protection.

17. Harvey Langi (LB) - Patriots

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2017 Still Active Harvey Langi has been a solid linebacker with the New England Patriots, known for his special teams play and versatility.

18. Bronson Kaufusi (DL) - Ravens

Drafted: 3rd Round, 70th Overall in 2016 Retired: 2018 Bronson Kaufusi had a brief NFL career with the Ravens, spending time on the practice squad and as a rotational player before retiring early.

19. Ryker Jemmett (OL) - Bengals

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2023 Still Active Ryker Jemmett has quickly become a notable offensive lineman, helping protect quarterbacks and contribute to the Bengals' offensive line rotation.

20. Thomas Poole (WR) - Panthers

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2020 Still Active Thomas Poole has showcased his skills as a wide receiver, contributing on special teams and working his way into the Panthers' depth chart.

21. Tarren Streeter (WR) - Seahawks

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2021 Still Active Tarren Streeter spent some time on the Seahawks' practice squad and has gained recognition for his speed and catching ability, working to make an impact in future seasons.

22. Jason Goff (DE) - Vikings

Drafted: 5th Round, 145th Overall in 2020 Still Active Jason Goff was known for his pass-rushing abilities and for providing defensive depth as a defensive end. He contributed to the Vikings' rotation on defense.

23. Emory Jansen (LB) - Cowboys

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2021 Still Active Emory Jansen played linebacker with a solid work ethic, making his mark on special teams before receiving recognition for his all-around game.

24. Tanner Fyfe (DT) - Jaguars

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2021 Still Active Tanner Fyfe's versatility on the defensive line helped him earn a roster spot as a deep rotation player for the Jaguars, contributing in the trenches.

25. Zach Wilson (QB) - Jets

Drafted: 1st Round, 2nd Overall in 2021 Still Active Zach Wilson was drafted as a highly touted quarterback but has struggled with consistency and performance issues early in his career. Despite flashes of potential, his tenure with the New York Jets has been tumultuous, and he remains a work in progress with opportunities to improve as both a starter and backup.

26. Justin Lowe (RB) - Lions

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2018 Retired: 2021 Justin Lowe showed flashes as a dynamic running back with the Lions, contributing primarily in special teams and as a rotational player before retiring early.

27. Bobby Jones (CB) - Cowboys

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2016 Retired: 2020 Bobby Jones made an impact as a cornerback in his brief NFL tenure, with contributions on defense and special teams for the Cowboys.

28. Michael Eylar (OL) - Falcons

Drafted: 7th Round, 230th Overall in 2017 Still Active Michael Eylar has been a steady contributor to the Falcons' offensive line, providing depth as a versatile lineman.

29. Thomas Wilson (CB) - Patriots

Drafted: 6th Round, 180th Overall in 2015 Retired: 2019 Thomas Wilson had a brief NFL career, mainly contributing on special teams and in a limited cornerback role during his time with the Patriots.

30. Anshul Tewari (OL) - Dolphins

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2020 Still Active Anshul Tewari has been part of the Dolphins' offensive line rotation, contributing as a backup guard and tackle while learning under experienced veterans.

31. Barry Jeffreys (DT) - Raiders

Drafted: 4th Round, 115th Overall in 2019 Retired: 2022 Barry Jeffreys was known for his quickness off the line and had a short but notable career as a defensive tackle for the Raiders before retiring early due to injuries.

32. Raiden Figueredo (OL) - Bengals

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2021 Still Active Raiden Figueredo has been making strides as a backup offensive lineman, contributing on special teams and helping the Bengals' line depth grow over time.

33. Leo Kimpa (WR) - Chargers

Drafted: 7th Round, 250th Overall in 2021 Still Active Leo Kimpa was a fast and reliable wide receiver who made his mark on the Chargers' practice squad, looking to break into the regular season roster in the coming years.

34. Joe Simmons (TE) - Steelers

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2022 Still Active Joe Simmons has provided solid depth at the tight end position, serving as a backup and occasionally making appearances as a blocker and receiving option.

35. Andrew Jessup (OL) - Packers

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2019 Still Active Andrew Jessup has contributed as an offensive line backup for the Packers, showing potential in developmental roles.

36. Joe Robertson (WR) - Eagles

Drafted: 5th Round, 148th Overall in 2014 Retired: 2020 Joe Robertson had a short-lived career as a wide receiver, spending time on various practice squads before transitioning to coaching roles.

37. Amir Fahmi (DE) - Saints

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2021 Still Active Amir Fahmi showed promise as a pass rusher and is working on establishing himself as a regular contributor to the Saints' defense.

38. James Mason (DT) - Buccaneers

Drafted: 5th Round, 167th Overall in 2017 Retired: 2021 James Mason contributed as a rotational defensive tackle for the Buccaneers but struggled to hold down a starting role throughout his career.

39. Joseph Harris (CB) - Jaguars

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2021 Still Active Joseph Harris is a cornerback who has shown strong potential but has spent most of his early career on the Jaguars’ practice squad and special teams.

40. Brent Perkins (RB) - Bears

Drafted: 6th Round, 200th Overall in 2018 Retired: 2021 Brent Perkins provided depth at the running back position but never became a key contributor for the Bears and eventually retired early from the NFL.

41. Leo Bailey (OL) - Colts

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2020 Still Active Leo Bailey has worked as a backup offensive lineman for the Indianapolis Colts and has yet to make significant contributions as a starter.

42. Daniel Lanou (WR) - Titans

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2022 Still Active Daniel Lanou has spent most of his career as a developmental player for the Titans, with limited opportunities on the field.

43. John Berger (OL) - Panthers

Drafted: 5th Round, 152nd Overall in 2019 Retired: 2021 John Berger was a promising offensive lineman who contributed in rotational roles but struggled to establish himself as a consistent starter before retiring.

44. Joseph Millis (FB) - Dolphins

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2018 Retired: 2021 Joseph Millis was a hard-nosed fullback who contributed in limited roles, mostly on special teams, before retiring.

45. Allen Peters (DE) - Rams

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2021 Still Active Allen Peters has worked his way onto the Rams’ defensive line, providing depth as a situational pass rusher.

46. Brad Briggs (TE) - Cowboys

Drafted: 7th Round, 250th Overall in 2017 Retired: 2020 Brad Briggs had a brief stint as a tight end for the Cowboys but struggled to stay on the field, contributing more on special teams.

47. Jerry Wright (OL) - Falcons

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2017 Retired: 2021 Jerry Wright served as a backup offensive lineman for the Falcons, providing depth to the line but struggled to secure a starting role before retiring.

48. Kevin Burns (LB) - Ravens

Drafted: 6th Round, 182nd Overall in 2015 Retired: 2020 Kevin Burns played primarily on special teams with the Ravens, with limited roles in the linebacker rotation. His career was hampered by injuries, leading to early retirement.

49. Walter Straub (DE) - Browns

Drafted: 7th Round, 224th Overall in 2018 Retired: 2022 Walter Straub had a brief but impactful role as a situational pass rusher for the Cleveland Browns. Despite being a late-round draft pick, he earned a few starting opportunities before retiring early.

50. Thomas Goff (OL) - Cardinals

Signed as Undrafted Free Agent in 2021 Still Active Thomas Goff has worked his way into a developmental offensive lineman role with the Arizona Cardinals, focusing on improving his technique to earn more opportunities on the field.
